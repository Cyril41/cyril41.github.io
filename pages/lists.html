ALINA ESENZHANOVA


For me applied artificial intelligence, analysis means understanding a language, and synthesis means generating a literate text. Solving these problems will mean creating a more convenient form of interaction between a computer and a person. I was interested in exploring the intersection points of NLP and gender equality issues. I recently found out that voice assistants differ in gender biases, a Unesco publication made me think about changing the actual language and its reflection in machine learning.

I studied museology at SPBU and now work at the Erarta Museum. I have always been interested in keeping the personal data of users and visitors and how the user giving his data to social networks can be influenced by the algorithms. It seems to me that soon we will be able to understand the state of mind of the user who left a review with the help of emotional intelligence and machine learning.
